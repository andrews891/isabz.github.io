Predictive Technologies

Landslide detecting device being fitted (Source: Reuters)

Improvements in computing has allowed scientists to predict when natural hazards will occur more accurately and reliably. In the past, it was virtually impossible to predict when disasters such as earthquakes would strike however nowadays computers are able to process realtime data from multiple sensors and detect patterns that would indicate disaster. These devices can save a lot of lives by warning people and the government to imminent threats to their lives and property ahead of time

Winter Road Safety

Gritting the road properly can be a very difficult task as humans are having to guess where to apply the grit. The advancement of computing has now enabled a tech company to develop a phone sized device that detects areas without any grit on roads. It uses radar to gather data from the road surface and then analyses the data to detect which areas of the road is most slippy. This technology will make the roads safer since there will be less slippy patches on the roads while also reducing the cost of gritting for tax payers as the grit is being applied more efficiently. Moreover this device can also detect potholes forming in the road, allowing them to be patched up before causing any damage to vehicles driving over it.
